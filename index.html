Dr Jeffrey C Hoffman
Head of Department: School of Nursing, FCHS

“It is critical in the Faculty of Community and Health Sciences (FCHS) to develop skills and competences that will allow our future health sciences professionals to be socially responsive to society's needs. As South African experts in skills development, we are no strangers to simulation and see the opportunity to engage with Artificial Reality (AR) & Virtual Reality (VR) as a natural evolution in an ever-changing environment driven by technology. Working with the University Innovation Hub (UIH) has thus been both a privilege and an inspiration. The opportunity allows us as a faculty to build from strength to strength and become leaders in skills development. In the School of Nursing (SON), for example, we established a fundamental competency that is an absolute prerequisite to learn and use namely the Emergency Trolley. The UIH and its revolutionary technology enabled us to transform the emergency trolley into an augmented reality instructional tool, instilling and advancing students' abilities to work towards competency in emergency care in the comfort of their own space and time. Providing and permitting students to interact in and access educational material that was previously only available on rotation and during designated time periods behind the doors of simulation laboratories. Further the UIH, equips health care professionals (academics) with cutting-edge technology that enables them to think imaginatively when developing educational activities and opportunities. Through a trans-disciplinary approach, the UIH and team of specialists alter the potential of skill development in the FCHS. Their (the UIH team's) expertise and commitment are remarkable and recommendable, making collaboration simple.”

Shahrazaad Hassan-Yengopal
PHD Student: Dentistry Faculty

“Clinical skills (like in dentistry) are competency based, and we don’t always have live patients to practice on, and each patient is different. Students don’t get the tactile feel or ‘force-feedback’ when they are working on manikins or orthodontist. Therefore, just like Nursing is trying to enable practice without harming a patient, we are linking with the Hub studio to look at the development of a suitable haptic device for Dentistry students”

Frequently asked questions we get from some fo our visitors if you have any questions or need any information please feel free to contact us

HOW MANY PEOPLE CAN JOIN A TOUR AND TRAINING SESSION?

Tour group sizes range from 1 - 15 per tour. Larger group bookings can be arranged upon request:

ARE YOU ALLOWED TO DANCE ON THE EQUIPMENT?

This depends on your dancing. Are you a shuffler? Go ahead and shuffle. Are you an interpretative dancer? Try the Interactive mirror, but perhaps none of the other equipment is applicable.

CAN I WEAR VR GLASSES A FASHION PIECE?

You could, but you really shouldn’t. VR glasses are designed for specific equipment and should always be used for that equipment only.

IS VIRTUAL REALITY REAL AND CAN IT TELL MY FUTURE?

You make your own destiny. At best, virtual reality can help you bring the future closer.

WHAT SHOULD I WEAR?

The only thing we ask is that you avoid high heels please. We like all kinds of other clothes though, so please wear whatever else you like!
